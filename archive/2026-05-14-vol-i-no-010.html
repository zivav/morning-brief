SUMMIT OPENS
IN BEIJING

Trump and Xi met at the Great Hall of the People on May 14 — the first US presidential state visit to China since 2017. Xi opened with a direct question: can the two powers avoid the "Thucydides Trap"? The pre-summit tariff architecture set the floor: US duties cut to 30% on Chinese goods, China to 10% on US goods, with a 90-day review window.

Expected deals under negotiation Day 1:
  • Boeing — 500-aircraft purchase; Kalshi traders pricing 86% probability of announcement
  • Soybeans — 25M metric tons/year long-term agricultural import commitment
  • H200 chip framework — case-by-case licensing pathway; could restore $3.5-4B annual Nvidia China revenue
  • Rare earths — China partial relaxation of gallium, germanium, antimony, graphite export restrictions
  • Board of Trade — USTR Greer's bilateral mechanism to formalize managed trade flows
"Many good things have been achieved." — Elon Musk, exiting the Great Hall of the People · May 14, 2026 (CNBC)

PIPELINE DETONATES

PPI April surged to +6.0% YoY vs the +4.9% consensus — the hottest wholesale inflation read since December 2022. The MoM print was +1.4% vs a +0.5% estimate, the largest monthly gain since March 2022.

What drove it:

  • Gasoline +15.6% MoM — Iran war Hormuz supply shock flowing through pump prices
  • Final demand energy +7.8% MoM — broadest energy surge since 2022
  • Trade services +2.7% MoM — tariff pass-through accelerating into margins
  • Core PPI +5.2% YoY vs +4.3% est — impulse not purely commodity-driven

WARSH TAKES COMMAND

Senate confirmed Kevin Warsh as the 11th Fed Chair on May 13 in a 54-45 vote — narrowest confirmation in the modern Fed era. Only one Democrat crossed over (Sen. Fetterman, PA). Powell's term expires May 16; he remains as Governor — an arrangement not seen in ~80 years.

The inflation gauntlet Warsh inherits:

  • CPI April: +3.8% YoY — hottest since May 2023
  • PPI April: +6.0% YoY — hottest since Dec 2022
  • PCE: ~4.5% annualized (est.)
  • Brent: ~$108 — Iran war Hormuz supply shock
  • TNX 10-yr: 4.49% — highest since July 2025
  • Rate hike probability by Dec 2026: 30-35% (CME FedWatch)
  • Zero cuts in 2026: ~58% probability

HUANG IN THE ROOM

NVDA CEO Jensen Huang was initially absent from the Beijing delegation — an absence the market briefly priced as a signal of limited chip-policy ambition. Trump called him personally; Huang boarded Air Force One in Anchorage. His presence in the Great Hall is the clearest possible signal that AI chip export policy is the summit's strategic substrate.

The developing H200 licensing framework targets a "presumption of approval for commercial AI research" — restoring an estimated $3.5-4B in annual China GPU revenue. This adds a second catalyst on top of the fundamental May 20 earnings setup.

May 20 earnings setup:
