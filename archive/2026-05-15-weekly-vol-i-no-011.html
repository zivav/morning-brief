CHAPTER VI · WHAT DIDN'T WORK · BRENT SHORT

THE DEAL THAT
NEVER CAME

The BRENT short assumed Iran deal closure end-May (Citi thesis). Every development this week moved against the position. −2.3% loss from $104.49 flag as Brent closed $106.89 on May 15.

WHY IT FAILED — 7 FACTORS
  • Trump called Iran's 30-day ceasefire proposal "piece of garbage" — the thesis' timeline assumption collapsed before markets opened Wednesday
  • Operation Sledgehammer contingency planning leaked; Pentagon signaling escalation intent, not resolution
  • IEA revised supply deficit outlook through October — this is not a short-term disruption; it's structural through Q3 2026
  • 4M bbl/day Hormuz flow loss cannot reverse quickly even with a deal — 4–8 weeks for shipping normalization means no rapid Brent relief
  • Beijing summit focused on Iran/Taiwan, not Hormuz mediation; the key multilateral pathway for deal facilitation remained closed
  • OPEC+ deployed no spare capacity — no demand-side offset, no IEA reserve release announced; supply gap widened
  • Citi's "end-May" thesis has been wrong for three consecutive weeks; credibility of the specific timing estimate is now severely damaged
HONEST ASSESSMENT
The direction may still be correct — deal will come eventually and Brent will normalize. The timing assumption was wrong. At $104.49, Brent was already pricing ceasefire-at-risk. The short needed de-escalation signal within 7 days. That signal didn't come. The position closed as a loss and the thesis is demoted to watch, not trade.
