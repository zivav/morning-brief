CHAPTER IV · BRENT CRUDE ESCALATION MAP · APR–MAY 2026

OIL SHOCK — BYPASS CLOSED

Brent's trajectory since the Iran war began tells the structural story: a brief peace-bid correction from $126 down to $104, then a steady regrind to $111+ as Hormuz stayed shut, and now a +6% spike to $114.44 as Iran attacks the Fujairah bypass — eliminating UAE's primary export workaround.

What changed Monday: Fujairah sits at the end of the Abu Dhabi Crude Oil Pipeline — the ~400km overland pipe built precisely to route crude to the Gulf of Oman without passing through Hormuz. By targeting Fujairah, Iran closes the bypass, leaving no alternative export route for UAE crude.

  • Supply disruption — IEA called it "largest supply disruption in history" when Hormuz first closed
  • Next catalyst — US military response to ceasefire break; any diplomatic channel that reopens
  • Watch — $120 Brent level triggers global recession modeling at major banks
  • TASE note — Israel's TA-125 at 4,371 (+70.71% YoY); defense export demand provides partial insulation from energy shock
