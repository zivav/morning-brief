I · MACRO · 01 MAY 2026

PRICES ERUPT
WARSH INHERITS

ISM Manufacturing PMI held at 52.7% in April — but the headline buried the lead. The Prices Paid component vaulted +6.3 pp to 84.6%, the single-largest monthly jump since the 2021–22 commodity shock (ISM, May 1).

  • Prices Paid — 84.6% (from 78.3%) · +6.3 pp · highest since early-2022 energy/commodity spike
  • New Orders — 54.1% (from 53.5%) · fourth straight expansion month
  • Expansion streak — 18 consecutive months of manufacturing growth
  • Implication — Input cost re-acceleration; core goods disinflation reversal risk

Kevin Warsh inherits a committee in open revolt against easing. Senate Banking advanced his nomination on a party-line vote; he takes over May 15. Powell stays on as governor — an unprecedented dynamic last seen in 1979.

  • Four dissenters at Apr 29 FOMC — Hammack, Kashkari, Logan against easing bias; most since Oct 1992
  • 2026 rate cuts — fully priced out; early-2027 hike probability rising
  • Warsh's bind — PCE +4.5%, Prices Paid 84.6%; no economic cover for easing
  • Powell as governor — potential parallel power center; unprecedented since Fed-Treasury Accord era

PEACE BID
WITHOUT NUKES

Iran delivered a new peace proposal to US mediators via Pakistani officials, signaling willingness to reopen the Strait of Hormuz in exchange for lifting the US port blockade. Brent fell −2.1% to $108.10 on the news — 14% below last week's $126 intraday peak (CNBC, May 1). The Strait remains closed; ship traffic is far below pre-war levels.

  • Iran's offer — Reopen Hormuz in exchange for lifting US port blockade; nuclear programme excluded from proposal
  • Trump's response — Rejected as insufficient; "unhappy" the deal doesn't include Iran's nuclear file (CNN, May 1)
  • Ceasefire status — In effect since Apr 7; no direct US-Iran fire; War Powers Resolution technically satisfied
  • Hormuz traffic — Remains far below pre-war norms; ~20% of global oil trade affected
  • IRGC threat — Senior IRGC official warned of "long and painful strikes" if US renews attacks
  • WTI — ~$104/bbl; set for second consecutive weekly gain on stalled peace talks
